Session 2026

Admissions Open

Join a legacy of excellence. Bahria College Karsaz welcomes applications for the new academic session across all wings and programmes.

Bahria College Karsaz invites applications for Session 2026 across its Higher Secondary, Cambridge O-Level, A-Level, Secondary, Middle, Primary, and Junior Wings. Admissions are announced through national newspapers and the college's official social media channels (Facebook, Instagram & WhatsApp), and are granted strictly on merit, subject to the availability of seats and the qualifying entrance test.

For Higher Secondary (HSSC) classes, a minimum of 65% marks in Matric (SSC) is required for general stream admissions, while 70% marks are mandatory for the Cambridge O-Level stream. Selection for HSSC is determined by a combined formula of 60% weightage on Class IX percentage and 40% weightage on the entrance test score. All admissions are provisional until candidates secure the required minimum marks in their final SSC or equivalent results.

Requirements

Eligibility Criteria

Meet the minimum standards for your chosen programme. All admissions are subject to seat availability and a qualifying entrance test.

HSSC Admission 65%

Minimum marks in Matric (SSC) required for admission to Higher Secondary (Classes XI–XII) in all general stream disciplines.

O-Level Stream 70%

Minimum marks required in the qualifying entrance test (in aggregate) to apply for Cambridge O-Level and A-Level programmes.

HSSC Selection Formula 60/40

Merit is calculated as 60% weightage on Class IX percentage plus 40% weightage on the HSSC entrance test score.

Step by Step

How to Apply

Follow the steps below to complete your application. Admissions are announced through newspapers and the college's official social media accounts.

1

Watch for the Official Announcement

Admissions for HSSC (Class XI) are announced in April/May each year through national newspapers and the college's official social media channels — Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp Channel. Admissions for Secondary and Middle Wing afternoon shifts are similarly announced prior to the new academic session. For Primary and Junior Wings, no formal notification is issued; admissions are processed on a rolling basis subject to seat availability.

2

Obtain the Prospectus & Application Form

Collect the admission prospectus and application form from the college's admissions office during office hours. The prospectus details all available programmes, disciplines, fee structures, and entry requirements for each wing and class level.

3

Complete the Application Form

Fill in the application form carefully and completely. Ensure all personal, academic, and guardian details are accurate. Select your preferred programme or discipline (e.g. Pre-Medical, Pre-Engineering, Computer Science, Commerce, or Humanities for HSSC; O-Level or A-Level for the Cambridge Wing).

4

Attach All Required Documents

Submit the completed application form together with attested photocopies of all required documents. Ensure originals are available for verification at the time of submission. Incomplete applications or those with missing documents will not be processed.

5

Appear in the Entrance Test

All applicants must sit the college entrance test. For HSSC, subjects tested are based on the chosen discipline — Pre-Engineering candidates are tested in English, Physics, Chemistry and Math; Pre-Medical in English, Physics, Chemistry and Biology; Computer Science in English, Physics, Chemistry and Computer/Biology; Humanities in English, General Math and General Science; and Commerce in English and Math. A minimum of 60% marks per subject is required to qualify for Middle and Secondary Wings; 70% in aggregate for Cambridge stream. HSSC merit is calculated as 60% Class IX percentage + 40% entrance test score.

6

Check the Merit List

Merit lists are displayed at the college notice board and official social media accounts. Shortlisted candidates are ranked strictly on merit. Students who qualify the entrance test and meet the eligibility criteria are called for admission in order of merit, subject to available seats in their chosen discipline.

7

Complete Admission & Deposit Fee

Selected candidates must report to the admissions office within the stipulated time to complete the admission formalities and deposit the prescribed fee. Failure to deposit the fee within the given deadline will result in the forfeiture of the allocated seat, which will be offered to the next candidate on the merit list.

8

Provisional Admission & Final Confirmation

HSSC admissions are provisional until the candidate secures a minimum of 65% in SSC/equivalent results. Students admitted to the afternoon shift will not be transferred to the morning shift under any circumstances. For Cambridge O-Level, students showing academic deficiencies in coping with the O-Level curriculum may be transferred to the Matric stream, subject to available vacancies, up to Class VIII only.

Please Read Carefully

Important Notes

No Transfer from Afternoon to Morning Shift

Students once admitted to the afternoon shift will not be transferred to the morning shift under any circumstances. This applies to both the Higher Secondary Wing (HSSC) and the Middle/Secondary Wings.

HSSC Admissions are Provisional

All HSSC admissions are provisional. Final confirmation is subject to the candidate securing a minimum of 65% marks in their SSC (Matric) or equivalent final result. Candidates who fail to meet this requirement may have their admission cancelled.

No Admissions in Class X

No admissions are announced for Class X in any shift or stream. Candidates must apply at the Class IX level to join the Secondary Wing.

Morning Shift Seats Rarely Available

For Middle and Secondary Wings, morning shift seats are typically not available. Admissions to the morning shift are only processed if seats become vacant, and no formal notification is issued for morning shift openings.

O-Level Stream Transfer Policy

Students in the Cambridge O-Level stream who show academic deficiencies and clear signs of difficulty in coping with the curriculum may be transferred to the Matric stream, subject to seat availability. Such transfers are only possible up to Class VIII of the O-Level (P-III level). No transfers are entertained from O-Level to Matric stream at Class IX level or above.

Montessori Age Requirement

Admissions to Montessori Beginners are restricted to children aged 2.25 to 3.6 years. Overage students are not entertained. Montessori admissions are generally conducted in December/January, subject to approval from higher authorities and availability of seats.
